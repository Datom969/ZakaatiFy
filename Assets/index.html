Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common Zakaat questions

What is Zakaat?
Zakaat is an obligatory charity in Islam, required from Muslims who meet the Nisab threshold. It purifies wealth and supports those in need.
What is Nisab?
Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must own for a full lunar year before Zakat becomes obligatory.
How is Nisab calculated?
Nisab is calculated based on the current market value of gold or silver. The Hanafi madhhab uses silver, while others typically use gold.
When is Zakaat due?
Zakaat becomes due once your wealth remains above the Nisab threshold for one full lunar year (Hawl).
Who can receive Zakaat?
Zakaat can be given to the poor, needy, those in debt, travelers in need, and other eligible categories defined in the Qur’an.
What's the difference between Zakaat and Sodaqoh?
Zakaat is an obligatory charity- (once you have the financial capability) with a set amount, while Sodaqoh is voluntary and given anytime in any amount.
